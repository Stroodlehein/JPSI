The case for a domestic physical metals benchmark

The Problem with Spot Price

Financial markets price paper claims on metals — leveraged futures contracts, synthetic supply, and institutional settlement markets. COMEX and LBMA spot prices represent what institutions agree to pay each other for metal they may never physically hold.

But in Japan, what actually matters is the yen-per-gram price you can buy or sell real metal for today — at a bullion dealer's counter, a pawn broker's window, a recycler's intake desk, or on Mercari between private individuals. These are not the same market. They never were.

Global spot ≠ Japanese physical reality. Retail participants don't transact at "spot." They transact at Tanaka's bid, Tokuriki's buyback rate, Nanboya's Sv1000 price, and Mercari's peer-to-peer premiums.

Japan's physical metals market is fragmented, opaque, locally priced, and often decoupled from global futures prices. It is subject to domestic demand surges, scrap supply constraints, dealer competition, and retail hoarding dynamics that global benchmarks simply do not capture.

Mission

JPMI aggregates real domestic dealer bids, pawn buyback rates, recycler payouts, and peer-to-peer transaction data to produce a live reference price for physical metals in Japan — reflecting retail liquidity, domestic demand, real buy/sell spreads, scrap versus bullion flows, and secondary market premiums.

The index is expressed in JPY per gram — the unit that Japanese retail participants actually use — not USD per troy ounce, which is the convention of a market most domestic holders never directly access.

The Paradigm Shift

JPMI represents a deliberate move away from paper-market conventions toward the reality of domestic physical metal ownership.

Index Structure — Three Layers

The JPMI composite is built from three distinct layers of the domestic physical market, each capturing a different tier of price discovery.

RS
Refinery Spot
The upstream institutional floor. What Japan's major precious metal refiners will pay to buy back silver from the public. The most transparent and consistently published domestic price tier.
Tanaka 田中貴金属
Nihon Material 日本マテリアル
Mitsubishi 三菱マテリアル*
DB
Dealer / Pawn Bid
The retail exit liquidity layer. What pawn shops and kaitori dealers will pay walk-in customers for physical silver. Reflects real-world liquidation value for ordinary holders — often below RS, but a truer signal of accessible liquidity.
Nanboya ナンボヤ
Daikichi 買取大吉
SR
Street Retail
The peer-to-peer premium signal. Actual sold prices for .999 silver bullion coins on Mercari — what buyers willingly pay above refinery rates. When SR diverges sharply upward from RS, it signals retail hoarding, supply tightness, or a demand surge not yet reflected in institutional prices.
Mercari メルカリ
MSPI-B (1oz .999)

Calculation Methodology

The JPMI composite is a weighted average of the three sub-indices. Default weights reflect the relative reliability and update frequency of each data source, but can be adjusted by the user.

// RS — simple mean of refinery buyback prices
RS = mean(Tanaka_buy, Tokuriki_buy)

// DB — simple mean of dealer / pawn bid prices
DB = mean(Nanboya_Sv1000, Daikichi_1g)

// SR — mean of Mercari sold listings converted to ¥/g
SR = mean(sold_price_¥ ÷ 31.1035) // MSPI-B · 1oz .999 bullion only

// JPMI composite (default weights: RS 50%, DB 25%, SR 25%)
JPMI = (RS × wRS) + (DB × wDB) + (SR × wSR)

// Street premium — how far retail leads the refinery floor
Premium = (SR ÷ RS) − 1

The SR component uses only sold listings for standard 1oz .999 silver bullion coins — no jewellery, no slabs, no numismatic pieces — to ensure like-for-like comparison. This sub-index is referred to as MSPI-B (Mercari Silver Price Index — Bullion).

Data Sources & Update Frequency

RS and DB prices are fetched automatically once daily via GitHub Actions, which runs a Python scraper against the public-facing price pages of each source. Prices are stored in prices.json and read by the index page on load.

SR (Mercari MSPI-B) must be entered manually, as Mercari does not provide a public API. Users are asked to record recent sold listings for 1oz .999 bullion coins and enter the transaction prices. The index calculates per-gram value automatically.

* Mitsubishi Materials publishes a retail sell price and a savings-scheme buyback rate. Physical OTC silver buyback is not available at their stores. Their retail sell price is included as an RS reference point but is not counted in the RS buyback mean.
